Emergency contraception

Emergency contraception can stop you getting pregnant after having unprotected sex (sex without contraception) or when contraception might have failed.

You need to use emergency contraception within 3 to 5 days of having unprotected sex. The sooner you use emergency contraception, the more effective it usually is.

Information:

Emergency contraception is free from NHS services such as sexual health clinics and GP surgeries

Where can I get emergency contraception?

You can get free emergency contraception from:

  • most sexual health clinics, also called family planning or contraception clinics
  • most GP surgeries - you can contact your GP surgery for details
  • some NHS walk-in centres
  • pharmacies that offer NHS emergency contraception services

You can also buy the emergency contraceptive pill at most pharmacies. Many pharmacies also sell the emergency contraceptive pill online.

Find emergency contraception services

Types of emergency contraception

There are 2 main types of emergency contraception:

  • IUD (intrauterine device, also called the copper coil)
  • emergency contraceptive pill (also called the morning after pill)

The IUD is the most effective method of emergency contraception.

Though unlike the emergency contraceptive pill, you will need to make an appointment to have an IUD fitted by a by a nurse or doctor. This is usually done at a sexual health clinic or GP surgery.

IUD

An IUD can be fitted within 5 days (120 hours) after sex.

It is a small plastic T-shape that a nurse or doctor can put into your womb.

If you want, you can choose to keep the IUD fitted and use it as your usual method of contraception. An IUD lasts for 5 or 10 years, depending on the type.

If you do not want to keep it, the IUD can be removed after your next period

Read more about the IUD, including side effects and who can use it.

Emergency contraceptive pill

There are 2 main types of the emergency contraceptive pill use in the UK:

  • Levonelle, which needs to be taken within 3 days (72 hours) after sex
  • ellaOne, which needs to be taken within 5 days (120 hours) after sex

Read more about the emergency pill, including side effects and who can use it.
