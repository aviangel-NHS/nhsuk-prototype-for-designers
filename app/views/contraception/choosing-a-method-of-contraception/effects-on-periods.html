Effects on periods

Some types of contraception can affect your periods, making them lighter, heavier, more painful or less painful.

Help with painful periods

Some methods of contraception can help make periods less painful. These are:

  • combined pill
  • IUS (Mirena coil)
  • contraceptive implant
  • contraceptive injection

You may be offered 1 of these methods to treat period pain even if you do not need contraception to prevent a pregnancy.

Help with heavy periods

The IUS is generally the most effective type of contraception for helping with heavy periods.

Other methods that can help with heavy periods are:

  • combined pill
  • progestogen-only pill
  • contraceptive implant
  • contraception injection

You may be offered 1 of these to help with heavy periods even if you do not need to use contraception to prevent a pregnancy.

Can make periods heavier or more painful

Some women find their periods become more painful and heavier after having an IUD (copper coil) fitted. These side effects usually go away within a few months.

If side effects continue to be a problem then you can have your IUD removed. Talk to a GP or a doctor or nurse at a sexual health clinic for more advice.
