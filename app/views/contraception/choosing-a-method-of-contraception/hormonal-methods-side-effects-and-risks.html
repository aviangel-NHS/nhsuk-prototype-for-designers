Hormonal methods: side effects and risks

Some contraceptives used hormones to prevent pregnancy. Hormonal contraceptives tend to share the same common set of side effects.

Hormonal methods of contraception

Hormonal methods that contain oestrogen and progestogen are:

  • combined pill (the pill)
  • contraceptive patch
  • vaginal ring

Hormonal methods that contain progestogen only are:

  • progestogen-only pill (mini pill)
  • IUS (Mirena coil)
  • contraceptive implant
  • contraceptive injection

Side effects of hormonal contraception

Common side effects of hormonal contraception include:

  • headaches
  • feeling sick (nausea)
  • mood swings
  • weight gain
  • sore breasts or breasts that are painful to the touch
  • acne

These side effects should pass within around 3 months.

If side effects are a problem for you, contact a GP or sexual health clinic. A different type of contraception may suit you better.
