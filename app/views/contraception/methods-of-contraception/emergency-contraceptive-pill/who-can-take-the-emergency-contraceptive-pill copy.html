Who can take the emergency contraceptive pill

Can I take the emergency contraceptive pill?

You should be able to take the emergency contraceptive pill even if you cannot use other hormonal contraception, such as the combined pill.

The emergency pill might not work if you're very overweight with a body mass index (BMI) of 30 or more. You should be able to use an IUD (copper coil) as emergency contraception instead.

The emergency contraceptive pill may not be suitable for you if you need to take steroid medicines for asthma.

A doctor, nurse or pharmacist can advise whether the emergency contraceptive pill is suitable for you.


Medicines and herbal remedies that affect the emergency contraceptive pill (morning after pill)

Ask a doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking the emergency contraceptive pill if you take medicine to treat TB (tuberculosis), epilepsy or HIV.

You should also ask for advice if you take griseofulvin (an antifungal medicine), or St John's wort (a herbal remedy).

You may need to have an IUD fitted as an alternative method of emergency contraception.
