Taking the emergency contraceptive pill with other medicines

Some medicines, include herbal remedies, can affect how the combined pill (also called the pill) works and it may not work properly to stop you getting pregnant.

You may need to use another type of contraception.

Ask a doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking the pill if you take medicine to treat TB (tuberculosis), epilepsy or HIV.

You should also ask for advice if you take griseofulvin (an antifungal medicine), or St John's wort (a herbal remedy).
