How to take the emergency contraceptive pill (morning after pill)

How to take the emergency contraceptive pill

There are 2 main types of emergency contraceptive pill used in the UK:

  • Levonelle, which needs to be taken within 3 days (72 hours) after sex
  • ellaOne, which needs to be taken within 5 days (120 hours) after sex

How well does the emergency contraceptive pill work?

It is hard to estimate exactly how well the emergency contraceptive pill will work for you as this can depend on factors such as how quickly you took it after sex.

Research suggests that around 1 in to 2 women in every 100 who take an emergency contraceptive pill will get pregnant.

The most effective method of emergency contraceptive is the IUD (copper coil). Less than 1 in 1,000 women who use an IUD will get pregnant.

Read more about the IUD.


Breastfeeding after the emergency contraceptive pill

You'll need to pause breastfeeding after taking the emergency contraceptive pill.

Avoid breastfeeding your baby for:

  • 8 hours after taking Levonelle
  • 7 days after taking ellaOne

If you take ellaOne, you can use a breast pump to help you keep producing milk during the 7 days. But do not give the milk to your baby.
