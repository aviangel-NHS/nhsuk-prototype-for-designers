What is the combined pill?

  • The combined contraceptive pill (also called the pill) is a type of hormonal contraception containing the hormones oestrogen and progestogen.
  • It stops your ovaries releasing an egg each month and makes it harder for sperm to reach an egg.
  • When used correctly (perfect use), it stops 99 out of 100 women getting pregnant in a year.
  • If not used correctly (typical use, when mistakes can happen), it stops 91 out of 100 women getting pregnant.
  • When you stop taking the pill, your fertility usually returns to previous levels after about a month.
  • The pill can help with acne, heavy or painful periods, PMS (premenstrual syndrome) and endometriosis
