What to do if you miss or lose a pill

What to do if you miss 1 pill

If you have missed 1 pill anywhere in the pack, or you started a new pack 1 day late, you will still be protected from getting pregnant.

You should:

  • take the last pill you missed now, even if this means taking 2 pills in 1 day
  • carry on taking the rest of the pack as normal
  • take your 7-day pill-free break as normal, or if you're on an everyday (ED) pill, take your dummy (inactive) pills

You do not need to use another type of contraception.

Eloine, Qlaira and Zoely

The advice is different if you take Eloine, Qlaira or Zoely.

If you take one of these types of combined pill, read the patient information leaflet that comes with it. You can also find this information on the electronic Medicines Compendium (eMC) website:

  • Eloine - eMC
  • Qlaira - eMC
  • Zoely - eMC

What to do if you miss 2 or more pills

If you have missed 2 or more pills anywhere in the pack or started a new pack 2 or more days late (48 hours or more), there's a risk you could get pregnant.

You should:

  • take the last pill you missed now, even if this means taking 2 pills in 1 day
  • leave any earlier missed pills
  • carry on taking the rest of the pack as normal
  • use extra contraception, such as condoms, for the next 7 days

Depending on how many pills were left in the pack after the last missed pill, there is different advice on what to do when you finish the pack.

If there are 7 or more pills left in the pack

If there are 7 or more pills left in the pack after your last missed pill, finish the pack and:

  • take your 7-day break as normal – 21-day pill
  • take your dummy pills before you start your next pack – everyday (ED) pill

If there are less than 7 pills left in the pack

If there are less than 7 pills left in the pack after your last missed pill, finish the pack and start a new pack the next day.

This means that you:

  • do not have your pill-free break – 21-day pill
  • do not take your dummy pills – everyday (ED) pill

If you miss 2 or more pills in the first week of a pack, you may need to get emergency contraception if you've had unprotected sex in the last 7 days.

Eloine, Qlaira and Zoely

The advice is different if you take Eloine, Qlaira or Zoely.

If you take one of these types of combined pill, read the patient information leaflet that comes with it. You can also find this information on the electronic Medicines Compendium (eMC) website:

  • Eloine - eMC
  • Qlaira - eMC
  • Zoely - eMC

What to do if you lose a pill

If you take the 21-day pill

If you've lost 1 of your 21-day pills:

  • take the last pill in the packet instead
  • take the rest of the pills on their correct days
  • you'll reach the end of your packet sooner, but this will not affect your contraception
  • take your 7-day break as usual

This will mean starting your next packet on a different day of the week from usual.

Or, to avoid starting your next packet on a different day:

  • take a pill from another packet to replace the pill that's missing
  • take all the remaining pills as normal on their correct days
  • keep the packet you took the pill from as a spare, in case you lose any more pills

If you take the everyday (ED) pill

You need to know which pills are active and which are the dummy (inactive) pills. They are different colours.

If you've lost 1 active pill:

  • take the last active pill in the packet instead
  • take all the remaining active pills as normal on their correct days
  • you'll reach the inactive pills sooner, but this will not affect your contraception

This will mean starting your next packet on a different day of the week from usual.

Or, to avoid starting your next packet on a different day:

  • take an active pill from another packet to replace the pill that's missing
  • take all the remaining pills as normal on their correct days
  • keep the packet you took the pill from as a spare, in case you lose any more pills

If you've lost 1 dummy (inactive) pill:

  • do not take a pill to replace it
  • continue taking the remaining inactive pills on their correct days (your contraception will not be affected)
  • you'll start your next packet on the usual day
