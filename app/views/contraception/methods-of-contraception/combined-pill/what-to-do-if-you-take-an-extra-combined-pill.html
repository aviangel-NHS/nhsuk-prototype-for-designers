What to do if you take an extra combined pill

If you've taken any extra combined pills, carry on taking the rest of your packet as normal. Take them at the usual time you take your pill each day.

If you've taken 1 extra contraceptive pill, you will not have any symptoms and do not need to get medical advice.

If you've taken several extra pills, you may:

  • feel slightly sick
  • be sick (vomit)
  • have some vaginal bleeding

These symptoms usually will pass. You do not need to get medical advice unless your symptoms are severe.

If a child has taken any contraceptive pills, they may feel sick or be sick (vomit). If you're concerned, talk to a pharmacist or a GP, or contact NHS 111.


Correcting the days

If you've taken an extra pill, you won't be in line with the days on your pill packet.

To find out how to correct the days on your packet, read What to do if you lose a pill.
