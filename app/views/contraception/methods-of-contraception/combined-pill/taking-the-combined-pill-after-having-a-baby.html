Taking the combined pill after having a baby

Starting the pill after giving birth or when breastfeeding

If you have just had a baby and are not breastfeeding, you can usually start the pill on day 21 after the birth. But check with a doctor first.

If you're breastfeeding, you're advised to wait 6 weeks after giving birth before you start taking the pill. You will need to use a different method of contraception until then.

Whether you are breastfeeding or not, you will need to use another type of contraception, such as condoms, for 7 days after you start taking the pill.

For more information, read When can I use contraception after having a baby?
