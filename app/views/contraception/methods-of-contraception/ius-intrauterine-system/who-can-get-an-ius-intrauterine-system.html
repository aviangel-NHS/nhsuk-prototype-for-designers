Who can get an IUS (intrauterine system) Brand names: Mirena, Levosert, Kyleena, Jaydess

Can I get an IUS

Most people with a womb can use an IUS (intrauterine system), also called a hormonal coil.

A doctor or nurse will ask about your medical history to check if an IUS is suitable for you.

The IUS may not be suitable if you have:

  • breast cancer, or have had it in the past
  • cervical cancer or womb (uterus) cancer
  • a liver condition
  • unexplained bleeding between periods or after sex
  • a history of serious heart disease or stroke
  • a sexually transmitted infection (STI) or pelvic infection
  • problems with your womb or cervix, such as fibroids

If any of these apply to you, speak to the doctor or nurse about your contraception options.

IUS and medicines

An IUS is not affected by most other medicines, including prescription medicines, or herbal remedies.

An IUS may make some types of emergency contraception less effective. If you need emergency contraception while using an IUS, speak to your doctor or nurse for advice.

Getting an IUS after giving birth

An IUS can sometimes be fitted within 48 hours of giving birth. But you'll usually be advised to wait until 4 weeks after birth to have an IUS fitted.

It is possible to get pregnant starting from 3 weeks (21 days) after giving birth. It's important to use another form of contraception, such as condoms, until your IUS is fitted.

The IUS and breastfeeding

It's safe to have an IUS when you're breastfeeding, and it will not affect your milk supply.

Read more about contraception after having a baby.

Getting an IUS after a miscarriage or abortion

You can get an IUS straight after an abortion or miscarriage. You'll be protected against pregnancy straight away.
