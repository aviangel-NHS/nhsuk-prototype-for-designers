Side effects of an IUS (intrauterine system) Brand names: Mirena, Levosert, Kyleena, Jaydess

Side effects of an IUS

Side effects of an IUS (intrauterine system), also called a hormonal coil, are most common for a few months after having it fitted. Many IUS users do not get any side effects.

Possible side effects include:

  • pain and bleeding for a few days after the procedure
  • small fluid-filled sacs (cysts) on your ovaries â€“ these are usually harmless and disappear without treatment

Some people report headaches, acne, sore breasts, and mood changes. However, there isn't enough evidence to say whether this is caused by an IUS.

If you get any of these symptoms, they should go away within a few months. If any side effects are a problem for you, speak to a doctor or nurse.

Your partner should not be able to feel your IUS during sex. If they can, see a doctor or nurse for a check-up.

How an IUS affects periods

An IUS can significantly reduce menstrual bleeding, and even stop periods altogether for some users.

Some types of IUS are the most effective treatments for heavy periods.

However, you might get irregular, longer or more frequent bleeding for 3 to 6 months after having an IUS fitted. But this should improve within a few months.

If irregular or heavy bleeding continues for longer than 6 months, see your doctor or nurse for a check-up.

How well does an IUS work

An IUS is over 99% effective at preventing pregnancy.

This means that less than 1 in 100 women using an IUS as their method of contraception get pregnant each year.

Risks of the IUS

The IUS is very safe, but there are some rare risks, including:

  • pelvic infections, usually within 3 weeks of having an IUS fitted
  • your IUS moving or being pushed out by the womb, usually within 3 months of having an IUS fitted
  • damage to the womb
  • ectopic pregnancy in the rare case that the IUS fails

Urgent advice: Ask for an urgent GP appointment or get help from NHS 111 if:

You have an IUS and:

  • you have pain or tenderness in your lower abdomen
  • you have a high temperature
  • you have abnormal or smelly discharge
  • you have very heavy vaginal bleeding
  • you think you might be pregnant
  • you cannot feel the threads of your IUS, or they feel shorter or longer than usual

You can call 111 or get help from 111 online.

If you cannot feel your IUS threads

If you cannot feel the threads when you check for them you might not be protected against pregnancy, and you may need emergency contraception. The IUS can make some forms of emergency contraception less effective, so speak to your doctor, nurse or pharmacist for advice.

Use another form of contraception until a doctor or nurse has checked your IUS.
