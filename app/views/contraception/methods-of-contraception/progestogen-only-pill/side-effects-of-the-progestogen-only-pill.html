Side effects of the progestogen-only pill (mini pill)

Side effects of the progestogen-only pill

The progestogen-only pill (or mini pill) is generally very safe and has few side effects.

Some women find the mini pill changes their periods.

It can affect periods in different ways. Your periods may be lighter, more frequent or may stop altogether. You may get some bleeding (spotting) between periods.

Some users have reported acne, mood swings and weight gain, but there's not enough evidence to say whether this is caused by the mini pill.

Risks of the progestogen-only pill

There is a chance you could develop fluid-filled sacs on your ovaries (ovarian cysts) when taking the mini pill. These cysts are not dangerous and do not usually need to be removed.

The cysts usually disappear without treatment. In many cases, the cysts do not cause symptoms, although you may feel pain in the lower part of your tummy (pelvic pain).
