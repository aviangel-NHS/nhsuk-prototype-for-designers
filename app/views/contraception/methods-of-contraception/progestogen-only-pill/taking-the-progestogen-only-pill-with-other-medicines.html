Taking the progestogen-only pill (mini pill) with other medicines

Medicines and herbal remedies that affect the progestogen-only pill

Some medicines and herbal remedies can affect how the progestogen-only pill (or mini pill) works and it may not stop you getting pregnant.

You may need to use another type of contraception.

Ask a doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking the progestogen-only pill if you take medicine to treat TB (tuberculosis), epilepsy or HIV.

You should also ask for advice if you take griseofulvin (an antifungal medicine), or St John's wort (a herbal remedy).
