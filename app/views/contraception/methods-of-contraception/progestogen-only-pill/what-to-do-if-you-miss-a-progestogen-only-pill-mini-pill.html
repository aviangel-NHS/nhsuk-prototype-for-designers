What to do if you miss a progestogen-only pill (mini pill)

It depends on how much time has passed, and whether you take a traditional mini pill or a desogestrel mini pill.

If you're not sure, check the pack to see if your pills contain desogestrel, a type of progestogen.

Less than 3 hours (traditional) or 12 hours (desogestrel) late

If it's less than 3 hours after the time you usually take your mini pill (or less than 12 hours for desogestrel mini pills):

  • take your missed pill as soon as you remember
  • take your next pill at the usual time
  • you do not need to use extra contraception

If you had unprotected sex after you missed your pill, you do not need emergency contraception.

More than 3 hours (traditional) or 12 hours (desogestrel) late

If it's more than 3 hours after the time you usually take your mini pill (or more than 12 hours for desogestrel mini pills):

  • take a pill as soon as you remember – only take 1, even if you've missed more than 1 pill
  • take the next pill at the usual time – this may mean taking 2 pills on the same day
  • carry on taking your remaining pills each day at the usual time
  • use extra contraception such as condoms for the next 2 days (48 hours)

If you had unprotected sex after you missed your pill, you will need emergency contraception. Ask a pharmacist or doctor for advice.
