How to take the progestogen-only pill (mini pill)

When to take your progestogen-only pill

You need to take the progestogen-only pill (or mini pill) at around the same time every day for it to work properly.

There are 28 pills in a pack of mini pills. When you finish a pack, you start a new pack the next day. Do not take a break between packs.

There are 2 types of progestogen-only pill:

  • traditional mini pill
  • desogestrel mini pill

Whichever type you have, it's important to take 1 pill at the same time every day.

Traditional mini pill

If you forget to take your pill at the usual time, you can take it within 3 hours and still be protected against pregnancy.

For example, if you usually take your pill at 8am but you forget, make sure you take it by 11am that day.

Desogestrel mini pill

If you forget to take your pill at the usual time, you can take it within 12 hours and still be protected against pregnancy.

For example, if you usually take your pill at 8am but you forget, make sure you take it by 8pm that day.

Information:

For more information about how to take the mini pill, check the leaflet that comes with it, or speak to a doctor, nurse or pharmacist.

Starting the progestogen-only pill

If you're taking the mini pill for the first time, you can normally start taking it at any point in your menstrual cycle. The advice may be different if you have a short menstrual cycle (less than 24 days).

Speak to a doctor or nurse if you're not sure when to start taking the mini pill.

Starting on day 1 to 5 of your cycle

If you start the mini pill on day 1 to 5 of your menstrual cycle (day 1 is the first day of your period), you'll be protected from pregnancy straight away.

You do not need to use extra contraception such as condoms.

Starting after day 5 of your cycle

If you start the mini pill after day 5 of your cycle (more than 5 days after the start of your period), you will not be protected from pregnancy straight away.

It's possible to get pregnant from day 5 of your cycle, so you need to use extra contraception such as condoms. Keep using them until you have taken the mini pill for 7 days.

How well does the progestogen-only pill work?

The progestogen-only pill (or mini pill) is more than 99% effective.

When taking it correctly, less than 1 in 100 women will get pregnant in a year.

If not taking the mini pill correctly, 9 in 100 women will get pregnant. This is because mistakes can happen in real life. You might forget to take your pills or take them at the wrong time of day.

How to change to a different pill

If you want to change your contraceptive pill, ask a doctor or nurse at your GP surgery or sexual health clinic for advice, or speak to a pharmacist.

You'll usually need to start the new pill immediately.

You may need to use additional contraception such as condoms for 7 days, to give the new pill time to start working.

Information:

For advice about other types of contraception, speak to a doctor, nurse or pharmacist, or read about methods of contraception.
